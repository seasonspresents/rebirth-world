Why I Make Apparel

Rebirth Apparel carries the deeper message of what Rebirth is about. I collaborate with local, talented artists to bring each collection to life — from maté gourds to clothing. Every collection represents a different theme I'm currently resonating with. A message I'm living. Something I want to pass on.

These aren't basics. They're not trend pieces. Each collection is one of a kind — released once, for that season, for that chapter. When it sells out, it doesn't come back. The design, the story, and the moment belong to that drop.

My goal is simple: put something on your body that reminds you — and everyone around you — to embrace the change, enjoy the ride, and keep stepping into who you're becoming.

Collaboration

Rebirth × Tejidos de Santiagitos

During my service mission in Guatemala, I found myself beside Lake Atitlán — one of the most beautiful places on earth. In San Lucas Tolimán, I connected with an extraordinary group of Mayan women whose hands weave stories into fabric the way I shape stories into rings. This collaboration was born out of that connection.

This was a non-profit collection. Every piece was handwoven by these incredible women. The proceeds went directly back to support them and their community. It wasn't about product — it was about showing up for people who show up for each other.

  • 🪡
    Shoulder BagsHandwoven with traditional Mayan patterns and natural dyes from the region
  • 🧣
    Table RunnersBackstrap-loom woven textiles — each one carrying the weaver's individual touch
  • 👗
    Huipiles & FabricsTraditional woven garments from the Tz'utujil tradition of Lake Atitlán
"It was part art, part service, part love — for the people and the place. These women don't just weave fabric. They weave culture, community, and meaning into everything they make. Working alongside them was one of the great honors of my life."
