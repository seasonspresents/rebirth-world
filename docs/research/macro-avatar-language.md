# Satori AI — Tattoo Artist Macro-Avatar Language Banks

**Five voice-of-customer reference sheets extracted from real tattoo artist language online.** Every quote below is taken verbatim from tattoo artists, their spouses, industry forums, and artist blogs. Sources include: David Meek Tattoos blog, The Creative Independent (Gabrielle Widjaja interview), Tattooing 101 forum, Tyranicorn Tattoo blog, Painful Pleasures (Casey Hart interview), Stories & Ink, Electrum Supply blog, Threads posts, HubPages comments, MetaFilter, Bookedin/Porter testimonials, Electric Linda, KTREW Tattoo, Eden Body Art Studios, and Brilliance Tattoo. Grammar, typos, and profanity are preserved exactly as written.

## AVATAR 1: "THE DROWNING CREATIVE"

**Core Desire:** Reclaim creative time by eliminating booking and communication admin.

**Profile:** Tattoo artist drowning in DMs, emails, scheduling, and social media management. Resents becoming a full-time receptionist instead of the artist they trained to be. Spends 5+ hours/week on admin. Feels their art quality is suffering.

---

### 1. PROBLEM LANGUAGE

- "Nowadays artists are required to be posting on tik tok, Instagram, Facebook, Snapchat, Twitter, Reddit, etc etc and on and on… Lets not forget you have to draw your tattoos, then tattoo them."
- "Oh wait, do you have a family and hobbies? Well, crap when are you gonna have time for them if you're busy posting and scheduling and doing consultations in the DMs… its so much… wait, did you make your needle order? Did you want to paint today? Theres just too much going on."
- "DMs are far too Informal. We have lots of clients and if they all sent a DM and an email it's just too hard to keep up."
- "Responding to every single email while also drawing for current clients and tattooing for 8 hours a day is physically and creatively impossible."
- "you're supposed to be an artist, a therapist, a business owner, and a content creator?"
- "I get a lot (even when I put don't dm me) but I have mine turned off cause it's too much and I get overwhelmed. I prefer email!"
- "I've noticed a recurring theme around bookings, all being done through Social media DM's and or emails. A few artists I know mention what a pain this is when filling books due to the tediousness."
- "There are late nights, back-to-back appointments, conventions, creating and re-creating, managing social media and not to mention; running a business, ordering supplies, and accounting duties on top of the actual creative work. It's a lot."

### 2. DAILY EXPERIENCE LANGUAGE

- "I'm notoriously terrible at answering emails so I had to get an assistant to do it lol"
- "Sometimes going through emails can be a lot, especially if they start replying back immediately. Lots of messages come in and when you're focused on drawing and getting sleep, exercise and having a life outside of tattooing you want to also give the messages the right attention they deserve."
- "Our artists get an overwhelmingly large volume of emails that they respond to when they are not working directly with clients, and they typically respond to all their emails in batches when they open their books."
- "In addition to spending hours tattooing, their time is devoted to designing custom tattoos and drawing, managing their social media accounts, doing their bookkeeping and finances, purchasing supplies, and attempting to have a family and social lives."
- "Due to the busy schedules of tattoo artists, whether they're drawing, tattooing, responding to enquiries, doing their taxes, attempting to live a life outside of tattooing, time is precious"
- "Most tattooers spend 40 hours a week tattooing. Then the other tasks are left for the rest of our free time, which is why we're burnt out."
- "I put a whole lot of time and work into all of my designs, which forces me to spend nearly every single night and weekend at home working on drawings for people, unpaid."

### 3. BELIEF LANGUAGE

- "Too many artists think they need to be available all the time, and its simply not true."
- "We often feel like we can do everything on our own. And sometimes we can, but that doesn't mean we SHOULD."
- "most tattoo artists aren't struggling because of their art. They're struggling because their systems, communication, and visibility are letting them down."
- "for some it might be easy to fall into an overworking trap, especially if you like the work you're doing."
- "It felt like I was doing a disservice to my art to not do it full time."
- "For years, tattooing was sold as the dream job: do what you love, make your own hours, and build a career around art. But talk to enough mid-career tattooers and you'll hear a different story."

### 4. PSYCHOLOGICAL/MENTAL STATE LANGUAGE

- "There was a period where I just didn't have any idea what to make. I didn't even know what to draw for flash. I was feeling so jaded. I don't even know how to explain how depressed I was about my art. I was just like, 'Does this matter? Is this anything?'"
- "When I was in the worst of my burnout, I would dissociate completely from my art. I would try to draw something and be like, 'Who's drawing this right now? I'm not drawing this right now. I don't even know what this is.' It was just the weirdest feeling."
- "Spending years repeating trends (fine-line florals, infinity symbols, Pinterest flash) leaves some artists disconnected from their original love for the craft."
- "In and out of tech and full-time jobs, jumping in and out of tattooing part-time, doing freelance gigs part-time—it felt like my brain was splitting."
- "It's hard being an artist, man. It's a lot of working through your own stuff so that you can also work through your art."
- "The quiet exit of burned-out artists should be a wake-up call."

### 5. EMOTIONAL EXPRESSION

- **Fear:** "At certain points I was like, 'People have expectations for what Gentle Oriental is going to post.' I feel like I still work through that every day."
- **Fear:** "If the people are not on social media anymore, then what do we do? That's the problem that I'm dealing with right now."
- **Guilt:** "if your art is so much a part of you that you feel like you having issues is making your art have issues"
- **Hope:** "If I had one message for any artist going through burnout, it's that you will get through it. There will be a day that comes when creating doesn't feel so difficult anymore."
- **Relief:** "stepping back isn't failure. It might be the reset you need to come back stronger"
- **Determination:** "Fill your cup before you can fill your clients cups."
- **Determination:** "I was finally ready to pursue freelance and tattooing more and dive into investing in myself and my art more"

### 6. FAILED SOLUTIONS LANGUAGE

- "[Instagram] is dying right now. I also think people are really burnt out by social media."
- "I don't really have hopes anymore for things to go viral on Instagram the way it used to for me. I do feel like there's over-saturation in the market right now."
- "Before I was such a small account, like 800 followers, and I would just post whatever the fuck. I feel like I can't do that anymore."
- "'Hit me up' isn't a strategy. People need specific direction."
- "Due to the custom nature of our shop, we do not have an admin or office manager who books appointments."
- "Set aside 1–2 hours a week to do all your bookings, emails, and supply orders in one go. Saves your brain — and your time."

### 7. IDENTITY & RELATIONSHIP LANGUAGE

- "this is 'creative having to do admin and is overworked' vs 'bad at his job'"
- "tattoo artist got popular and is overwhelmed"
- "he's a creative that isn't great on organization skills"
- "Anyone who is making very identity-based art, if you're having an identity crisis, your art is going to be a little fucked for a little bit."
- "Gentle Oriental is just going to make whatever the fuck I want."
- "identity-driven artists who hate admin"
- "Many are quietly stepping away—from studios, from conventions, even from tattooing altogether."

### 8. DISCOVERY/LEARNING LANGUAGE

- "Identifying what was important to me allowed me to set…. wait for it….. wait for it…. BOUNDARIES!"
- "If there is something you can outsource- accounting, supplies, managing appointments, social media, allow yourself to do it and instead of spending that newfound time on more work, spend it on yourself. You deserve that shit!"
- "I was right about the fact that if I just invested my full ass into making and thinking deeply about my artwork, it would pay back tenfold."
- "People don't know, but burnout can last years."

### 9. TRANSFORMATION LANGUAGE

- "Bookedin completely changed the way we book tattoos. It's clear, professional and takes care of the entire process from deposit to aftercare. Clients feel confident and we finally feel organised."
- "I gained nearly one third of my work day back. Bookedin handles appointments so I can focus on designs and clients."
- "Clients know exactly where to book and how to pay, so I can simply direct them to the page and keep the conversation about art."
- "I got back to doing more of the things I love to do, more of the tattoos I love to do and the clients I like to work with… and yeah, it was scary at first but wow what a difference in quality of life."
- "Because I've had more clarity on myself, I've had a lot more clarity on my artwork or what I want to be doing with it. And the ideas come flowing in a lot easier than they were."

### 10. AUTHORITY REFERENCE LANGUAGE

- "I've been seeing a ton of tattoo artists complain about work life balance. Its so wierd to see it all over social media."
- "I read something a while back that said something to the effect of…" *(typical informal citation style)*
- Tattoo artists rarely cite formal experts — they reference peer artists, their own experience, and community consensus by name.

### 11. TIMELINE/REGRET LANGUAGE

- "Gone are the days of sitting in a tattoo shop waiting for walkins 6 days a week and letting word of mouth do all the work."
- "my daughter enters the teen years that hit really close to home… my major time as a dad is almost over. Not cool."
- "I was dealing with some major burnout in 2022 and 2023. That was the worst period creatively for me."
- "Me and my art were very far apart during that period of burnout, and slowly they shift back together until they're one cohesive blob."

### 12. HIGH-FREQUENCY WORDS

## AVATAR 2: "THE LEAKING PIPELINE"

**Core Desire:** Stop losing clients and revenue to slow or missed responses.

**Profile:** Artist hemorrhaging revenue because they can't respond fast enough. Messages get buried, inquiries go unanswered, clients book elsewhere. Knows they're leaving money on the table but feels stuck.

---

### 1. PROBLEM LANGUAGE

- "Instagram hides messages, and people could be reaching out to you, and you just have no idea."
- "Unfortunately some tattooers also get so many requests they can't respond to everyone if it's not an idea they want to do."
- "Messages get buried. Inquiries get missed, and important conversations can disappear without notice."
- "If your booking system requires a scavenger hunt, people will give up."
- "If you can't consistently find and respond to booking requests, you lose time, money, and trust."
- "A popular artist can receive hundreds of inquiries a week."
- "pro tip, call the shop and ask for the artist, let them know you're wanting to book. alot of the time your messages/emails end up buried behind hundreds of other spam emails, hundreds of other messages from people just wanting a price or a quote"
- "Booking is slower across the board - even for talented, established artists. Clients are canceling more often (money's tight everywhere)."

### 2. DAILY EXPERIENCE LANGUAGE

- "Emails and messages can get lost, especially when we have a lot going on. 9/10 times it's not intentional. I've opened messages, read them, then gotten sidetracked by a walk-in, and then forgotten to return to the message."
- "We can't be on our phones while we're tattooing, so 12 hours a day my phone is up yknow."
- "While some take emails year round, several of our artists only open their books to requests for work in a specific time window every few months, in order to condense their correspondence times."
- "if somebody fills in another form after two days it generates me more work."
- "My business is doing well and does not experience slow times. But I am working incredibly hard and far more hours than a person should have to work in order to keep up with the rising costs of living."
- "While his work inside the shop is still limited to 40 hours a week, Wessel spends another two to five hours per day working on drawings for his clients. This puts his work day at 10 hours, minimum."
- "I have worked with artists who felt it was normal/ok to take up to a month to reply."
- "Artists report inquiry deferral for weeks—only to reply late or not at all."

### 3. BELIEF LANGUAGE

- "There's a persistent myth in creative fields that great art excuses bad behavior. In the tattoo world, that often shows up as the 'quirky artist' who's impossible to pin down, slow to respond, or dismissive of basic boundaries."
- "Being unconventional doesn't mean being unreliable."
- "Professionalism isn't the opposite of creativity; it's what gives it room to breathe."
- "most tattoo artists aren't struggling because of their art. They're struggling because their systems, communication, and visibility are letting them down."
- "You're not falling behind because you're not talented. You're falling behind because the way you're running things is slowing you down."

### 4. PSYCHOLOGICAL/MENTAL STATE LANGUAGE

- "If you're feeling burnt out, under-booked, or just over chasing clients"
- "Supply costs are up, shop cuts can be steep, and clients increasingly price-shop online. For many, the math just doesn't work anymore."
- "If your shop feels quiet, your energy's low, and everything costs more — you're not imagining it. You're not alone. And you're definitely not failing."
- "Running a tattoo business—or just getting out of bed some days—feels heavier than it used to. The world is expensive, the internet is loud, and showing up every day to create something meaningful is no small thing."
- "Burnout doesn't mean you don't love tattooing—it just means you're human."
- "I worry about money: Always."
- "Broke, because I work in an industry with high costs to maintain quality and I am a one-man show."

### 5. EMOTIONAL EXPRESSION

- **Fear:** "worrying about missing an opportunity"
- **Fear:** "Otherwise, as history shows, art will decline as hardship grows."
- **Guilt:** "what if I'm a people pleaser and say yes way too often because I don't want to let anyone down?"
- **Hope:** "I'm so glad you guys found me. I don't even need to hire a studio manager anymore. It's been going SO well and we've 10000% been getting paid more and getting more bookings because of the app. My clients love It! I love It. Super grateful for you guys."

### 6. FAILED SOLUTIONS LANGUAGE

- "Social media isn't hitting the way it used to."
- "We are fortunate to receive more requests for tattoo work than we can accommodate at any given time, so please don't take it personally."
- "We have to turn away some work because we will not compromise quality or time spent with clients to take on a higher volume."
- "It's fine to send another email if you get no reply. Some tattooers aren't very organised."

### 7. IDENTITY & RELATIONSHIP LANGUAGE

- "our manager doesn't need to be a phone operator anymore. It's a no-brainer."
- "Being 'booked out for 6 months' isn't the only marker of achievement. A career that lasts 20+ years is the real flex."
- "Tattooing is more than a hustle—it's a career that requires sustainability."
- "Stepping back isn't failure."
- "If you're still here, still tattooing, still trying? That's not just survival. That's resilience. That's power."

### 8. DISCOVERY/LEARNING LANGUAGE

- "I also built a Google Form that allowed people to leave me all the info they thought I would need including reference photos and descriptions. I was able to pick and choose who I wanted to work with before they even came in."
- "You can even setup an autoresponder on Messenger and Instagram DM's that sends the link to people when they mention certain keywords like tattoo, appointment, quote etc."
- "Personally I have a fully digital booking process that I developed so I don't need to ask."
- "Your booking system should work for you, not against you."

### 9. TRANSFORMATION LANGUAGE

- "I'm so glad you guys found me. I don't even need to hire a studio manager anymore. It's been going SO well and we've 10000% been getting paid more and getting more bookings because of the app."
- "My income shot up after switching to Bookedin. Clients book and pay deposits without hesitation. It's like having a personal assistant who never sleeps."

## AVATAR 4: "THE EMPTY CHAIR"

**Core Desire:** Eliminate no-shows and protect revenue from cancellations and ghosts.

**Profile:** Artist who blocks off entire days for clients who never show up. Loses **$1,200+/month** to no-shows. Turned away other clients for someone who ghosted. Feels disrespected and financially vulnerable.

---

### 1. PROBLEM LANGUAGE

- "Chasing tattoo clients for deposits, dealing with last-minute cancellations, and watching no-shows eat up your day is exhausting."
- "Empty chairs cost tattoo artists more than just time. When a client doesn't show up to their appointment, you lose income that can't be recovered. After all, you can't sell yesterday's empty time slot tomorrow."
- "Cancellations suck, but they're part of the job. The difference is whether or not you get paid for the time you blocked off."
- "Also little bit annoys people who books time and never show up / always cancels. Usually they are the same people and my husband marks them on calender with the name 'question mark'"
- "We have a 48-hour cancellation deadline, but sometimes we get a call just a couple of hours before, or even right at the minute. Sorry, I can't make it. We made the design; we made everything ready for you."
- "Not letting your artist know you can't make your appointment means they've travelled into work, set up their station ready and have spent time drawing your design - all things they now won't be compensated for."
- "We also don't want to put you down for a date and time, expecting you to pay us $900, and then have you not show up. That's $900 of income we no longer have to pay our rent and feed our families."
- "You didn't start tattooing to become a customer service rep."

### 2. DAILY EXPERIENCE LANGUAGE

- "The average tattoo studio deals with no-show rates between 8-20%. For an artist charging $150/hour, that can translate to thousands in lost revenue every month."
- "Tattoo artists spend time preparing for each session, from creating custom designs to sanitizing equipment and setting up workstations. When a client cancels at the last minute, all that preparation goes to waste."
- "tattoo artists are self employed. This means that if you cancel your appointment outside of the accepted notice period, your artist is missing out on a day of work if they've set aside a full day for you. Without ample time, it can be difficult to find somebody else to fill your slot."
- "sick days don't exist so they try to keep from getting ill where it can be avoided."
- "A stray cancellation here or there isn't going to break the bank, but if they become normality, it will start to impact your bottom line."
- "Did that $300 tattoo take 5 hours because of setup, cleaning, and no-shows? Then you made $60/hr, not $150."
- "Stop juggling screenshots, DMs, and payment apps just to keep track of one booking."

### 3. BELIEF LANGUAGE

- "Some artists still feel weird asking for deposits. Don't. You're not being pushy—you're setting expectations for a professional service."
- "Most of these reasons aren't about clients deliberately wasting your time. They're normal human issues that can be addressed with the right systems."
