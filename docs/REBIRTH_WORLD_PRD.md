## 1. BRAND ORIGIN STORY

### 1.1 Who Is Daniel Malzl?

Daniel Malzl is the founder and sole craftsman behind Rebirth Rings. He is a skateboarder, jeweler, and entrepreneur currently based on the **North Shore of Oahu, Hawaii** (originally Provo, Utah).

**The Malzl Jewelry Lineage:**
Daniel comes from a family with deep roots in fine jewelry. His father, **Christoph Malzl**, is an Austrian master jeweler from **Salzburg** who trained at **Koppenwallner's** — the historic shop that crafted jewels for the Austrian emperor. Christoph completed a **six-year apprenticeship** learning old-world fabrication from raw sheet metal and wire (not modern wax casting). He later moved to Utah, graduated from BYU-Hawaii, married his Chilean wife Laura, and established himself in the Provo jewelry scene — what he called "the engagement capital of the world." Christoph's vision was always to make custom pieces for people who truly know and love jewels, and that DNA runs directly through Daniel.

**Daniel's Path:**
Daniel grew up in this jewelry-making world but came at it from the skate culture side. He's passionate about skateboarding, recycling, and creating. After snapping countless boards and never being able to throw them out, he one day came across a photo of a skateboard ring — and Rebirth Rings was born. The brand started in Provo, Utah, built community through the local skate scene, and eventually relocated to Hawaii's North Shore, where the surf and skate culture intersection creates the perfect brand environment.

**Key Personal Details:**
- Austrian-American heritage (Salzburg → Hawaii)
- LDS (Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints) background
- Father of three (Mattias, Melanie, Kiana)
- Skater, craftsman, entrepreneur
- Instagram: @daniel_malzl (personal), @rebirthrings (brand)
- Deep humanitarian drive (Mexico service projects, Samoa projects, breast cancer awareness, Rheumatic Rescue Foundation)

### 1.2 The Brand Name & Philosophy

**"Rebirth"** is not just a name — it's the entire philosophy:
- Broken skateboards are **reborn** as rings
- Waste materials find **new life** as wearable art
- The wearer embraces **transformation and change**
- The lotus 🪷 symbolizes growth through adversity

**Tagline:** *"Embrace Change 🪷"*
**Positioning Statement:** *"100% handmade recycled skateboard jewelry & apparel"*

## 3. THE EVOLUTION — WHERE WE'RE GOING

### 3.1 The Premium Pivot: Wood-Lined Metal Wedding Bands

The brand is scaling from pure recycled skateboard rings into **luxury wood-lined metal wedding bands**. This is the highest-leverage move for the business — the alternative men's wedding band market is booming, and Daniel's heritage gives him a competitive moat no one else has.

**Manufacturing Pipeline (In Progress):**

| Component | Source | Cost | Details |
|-----------|--------|------|---------|
| Metal Ring Shells | ARZ Steel (wholesale) | $15 steel / $18 gold or black | $3,000 minimum order, specific interior diameter per size |
| Wood Liners | RingSupplies.com, Craft Supplies USA | Varies | Irish bog oak, ancient bog oak, stabilized exotic woods |
| Adhesive | Starbond 3-pack (CA glue) | ~$25 | Thin, medium, thick + accelerator spray |
| Surface Prep | ForPro 91% Isopropyl Alcohol | ~$8 | Pure, no additives — critical for bond quality |
| Interior Finish | Thin CA glue (from Starbond kit) | Included | Superior to polyurethane for durability, water resistance, skin contact |

**The Process:**
1. Source metal ring shell from ARZ Steel (specific ID per target size)
2. Clean interior surface with 91% isopropyl alcohol
3. Bond stabilized wood liner inside using CA glue + accelerator
4. Target: **0.8mm wood liner** for slim, non-bulky profile
5. Finish interior with thin CA glue coat
6. Sizing: e.g., Size 9 shell (18.8mm ID) + wood liner → wearable size 7

**Price Point Target:** $75–$200+ (compared to current $25 skateboard rings)

**Demographics:**
- Male, 26–40 years old

- Values eco-consciousness — sustainability is a plus, not just a buzzword

### 4.3 Tertiary Avatar: "The Gift Giver"

- Someone shopping for a skater, surfer, or eco-conscious friend/partner/sibling
- Often female, buying for a male recipient
- Discovery: Gift guide content, Pinterest, Google "gifts for skateboarders"
- Price sweet spot: $25–$50 (current skateboard ring range)
- Key need: Easy to understand, easy to order, ships fast, looks great when gifted

## 5. BRAND AESTHETIC & DESIGN LANGUAGE

### 5.1 The Rebirth Aesthetic DNA

The brand lives at the intersection of four forces:

```
SKATE CULTURE (raw, handmade, colorful, community, authentic)
        ×
MINDFULNESS/TRANSFORMATION (lotus 🪷, "Embrace Change," zen, rebirth)
        ×
ECO/SUSTAINABILITY (recycled, upcycled, zero-waste, earth-first)
        ×
ISLAND CRAFT (North Shore vibes, natural textures, ocean tones, laid-back luxury)
```

### 5.2 Visual Identity Guidelines

**Color Palette:**

| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|------|-------|-----|-------|
| Primary Dark | Charcoal Black | `#1a1a1a` | Text, headers, dark backgrounds |
| Primary Light | Warm White | `#f5f0e8` | Backgrounds, light sections |
| Accent 1 | Ocean Teal | `#2a9d8f` | CTAs, highlights, links |
| Accent 2 | Burnt Amber | `#e07a3a` | Skateboard wood warmth, secondary CTA |
| Accent 3 | Lotus Pink | `#d4a0a0` | Feminine touch, gift guides, soft moments |
| Neutral | Driftwood Gray | `#8a8578` | Body text, secondary info, borders |
| Earth Tone | Moss Green | `#5a6b4a` | Eco/sustainability callouts |

**Typography Direction:**

| Role | Style | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Headlines | Bold, slightly condensed sans-serif | Think: confident, modern, slightly editorial. Avoid generic. Consider fonts like Satoshi, General Sans, Clash Display, or something with personality. |
| Body | Clean, readable serif or sans-serif | Warm and approachable. Consider DM Sans, Source Serif, or similar. |
| Accent/Callouts | Handwritten or organic feel | For quotes, taglines, personal touches — connects to the handmade ethos. |

**Photography Direction:**
- Natural lighting (golden hour, overcast diffused light)
- Lifestyle shots over sterile product shots — ring on a hand against ocean, skateboard, driftwood
- Texture-heavy: close-ups of wood grain, skateboard layers, metal finishes
- Warm, slightly desaturated color grading — not oversaturated Instagram filters
- Show the maker: Daniel's hands crafting, workshop shots, sawdust and tools

**Design Principles:**
1. **Handmade over polished** — imperfection is the aesthetic, not a flaw
2. **Story over specs** — lead with meaning, follow with materials
3. **Warmth over minimalism** — this isn't a cold tech brand, it's organic and human
4. **Authenticity over aspiration** — real photos, real stories, real craft
5. **Community over commerce** — the brand exists because people donate their broken boards

## 6. VOICE, TONE & COPYWRITING GUIDE

### 6.1 Brand Voice

**We sound like:** A wise, grounded skater friend who also happens to be a master craftsman. Not corporate. Not salesy. Not try-hard. Confident, warm, and real.

**We ARE:**
- Authentic and unpretentious
- Passionate about craft and process
- Environmentally conscious without being preachy
- Community-driven and generous
- Quietly confident — the work speaks for itself

**We are NOT:**
- Corporate or polished
- Discount-driven or pushy
- Trendy or hype-beasty
- Greenwashing or virtue-signaling
- Generic or template-sounding

### 6.2 Tone Spectrum

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Product pages | Warm, descriptive, crafted | "Seven layers of maple, shaped by hand from a board that once carved concrete. Each ring carries the story of a session that ended — and a new chapter that began." |
| Instagram captions | Casual, skater-adjacent, real | "This one came from a board donated by the homies at North Shore. The colors hit different when you know the story 🤙" |
| Wedding band copy | Elevated, emotional, meaningful | "Your ring should mean something. Ours are handmade from materials with a past — wood that's lived, metal that endures. Built for the moment you say 'forever.'" |
| Email marketing | Personal, direct, storytelling | "Hey — I just finished a batch of rings from some insane decks that came in last week. The layers on these are something else. Dropping Friday at noon." |
| VSL / Ad copy | Hook-driven, benefit-led, authentic | "Most wedding bands are made in a factory. This one was made by hand, from a broken skateboard, on the North Shore of Oahu." |

### 6.3 Key Phrases & Language

**Use freely:**
- Embrace Change
- Handmade with intention
- Reborn / Rebirth / New life
- Locally donated
- One of a kind
- Crafted (not manufactured)
- The story behind the ring
- Made with care / Made with passion

**Avoid:**
- "Luxury" (unless specifically about the wedding band line — and even then, use sparingly)
- "Cheap" or "affordable" (say "accessible" or just let price speak)
- "Eco-friendly" as a lead (it sounds like marketing — show, don't tell)
- "Handcrafted artisanal" (too pretentious)
- Any corporate jargon
