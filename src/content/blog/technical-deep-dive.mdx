# How to Care for Your Handcrafted Jewelry

Handcrafted jewelry is built to last — but a little care goes a long way. Here's everything you need to know to keep your Rebirth World pieces looking their best for years to come.

## General Care Tips

These apply to all jewelry, regardless of material:

- **Put jewelry on last** when getting ready. Apply perfume, lotion, and sunscreen before putting on your pieces.
- **Remove before water exposure.** Take off jewelry before showering, swimming, or doing dishes.
- **Avoid chemicals.** Household cleaners, chlorine, and harsh detergents can damage metals and finishes.
- **Wipe down after wearing.** A soft cloth after each wear removes oils and sweat that cause tarnish over time.

## Caring for Sterling Silver

Sterling silver naturally develops a patina over time. Some people love this aged look. If you prefer bright silver:

### Cleaning

1. Mix a few drops of mild dish soap with warm water
2. Dip a soft cloth or microfiber towel into the solution
3. Gently wipe the piece, paying attention to crevices
4. Rinse with clean water and pat dry immediately

For heavier tarnish, use a dedicated silver polishing cloth. Avoid abrasive cleaners or toothpaste — despite the old advice, they can scratch the surface.

### Storage

- Store in an airtight bag or jewelry box with anti-tarnish strips
- Keep pieces separated to prevent scratching
- Silica gel packets help absorb moisture

## Caring for Brass

Brass develops a warm, golden patina naturally. This is part of its character. To maintain the original shine:

### Cleaning

1. Make a paste of equal parts lemon juice and baking soda
2. Apply gently with a soft cloth
3. Rinse thoroughly with warm water
4. Dry immediately with a clean cloth

### Maintaining the Patina

If you love the aged look, simply wipe with a dry cloth after wearing. The natural oils from your skin will actually help develop a beautiful patina over time.

## Caring for Copper

Copper is the most reactive of the metals we work with, and it develops a green patina (verdigris) when exposed to moisture and air.

### Cleaning

1. Dip a cloth in white vinegar with a pinch of salt
2. Gently rub the piece
3. Rinse with water and dry thoroughly

### Preventing Green Skin

Some people experience a green mark on their skin from copper jewelry. This is harmless — it's a reaction between the metal and your skin's natural acids. To minimize it:

- Keep the piece dry
- Apply a thin coat of clear nail polish to the inside surface that contacts skin
- Remove before washing hands or exercising

## Storage Best Practices

Proper storage is the easiest way to extend the life of your jewelry:

- **Keep it dry:** Moisture is the enemy of all metals. Store in a low-humidity environment.
- **Separate your pieces:** Metals can scratch each other. Use individual pouches or compartments.
- **Avoid direct sunlight:** Prolonged UV exposure can affect certain finishes.
- **Hang necklaces:** This prevents tangling and kinking of chains.

If a piece is severely tarnished, bent, or a clasp is broken, reach out to us. We stand behind our craftsmanship and can advise on repair options.
